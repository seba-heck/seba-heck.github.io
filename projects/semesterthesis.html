Abstract

Traditional datasets, however, often rely on abstract, synthetic deformations that fail to capture the rich material properties influencing how objects behave under physical forces. This thesis addresses this limitation by introducing a novel dataset generation pipeline designed to create time-series data of multi-material de- formable objects. The dataset encodes both geometric information and material- specific properties by simulating deformations in a physics-based environment using MuJoCo. The pipeline leverages the PartNet dataset and is processed into tetrahedral volume meshes, suitable for simulation, using advanced meshing tech- niques like fTetWild. These meshes are then modelled in MuJoCo and simulated to induce realistic deformations. The resulting dataset captures the time evolution of these deformations, providing a valuable resource for training and evaluating machine learning models aimed at material recognition and robotic interaction.
